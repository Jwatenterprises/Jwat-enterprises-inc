Funding for Every Business Stage: A Complete Overview

1. Startup Business Loans

Starting a business without capital is one of the hardest positions to be in — but it's not hopeless. Startup loans are designed for businesses that are pre-revenue or in their earliest stages of operation. These loans are often based on personal credit, business plan strength, and projected revenue rather than existing financials.

Startup loans through platforms like ROK Financial can provide early-stage businesses with the seed capital they need to cover initial expenses including equipment, inventory, marketing, staffing, and operating costs. Amounts typically range from $5,000 to $150,000 for early-stage businesses, depending on qualifications.

2. SBA 7(a) Loans

The SBA 7(a) loan is the gold standard for small business financing. Backed by the U.S. Small Business Administration, these loans offer competitive interest rates, longer repayment terms, and higher loan amounts — up to $5 million. They're ideal for businesses with at least two years of operating history, solid revenue, and a clear use of funds such as business acquisition, real estate, equipment, or working capital.

SBA 7(a) loans require more documentation and have a longer approval timeline than other products, but the terms are among the most favorable available to small businesses. If you qualify, this should be a serious consideration.

3. SBA 504 Loans

The SBA 504 loan is specifically structured for major fixed asset purchases — think commercial real estate, large equipment, or facility improvements. These loans are typically structured with a bank covering 50% of the project, an SBA-backed Certified Development Company (CDC) covering 40%, and the borrower contributing 10% as a down payment.

SBA 504 loans are ideal for established businesses looking to own their building, upgrade major infrastructure, or make significant capital investments. Loan amounts can reach $5.5 million and beyond for certain projects.

4. Business Lines of Credit

A business line of credit gives you flexible, revolving access to capital up to a set limit. You draw only what you need, pay interest only on what you use, and replenish the line as you repay. This is one of the most powerful tools for managing cash flow, covering payroll gaps, handling unexpected expenses, or jumping on inventory opportunities quickly.

Lines of credit are available from $10,000 to $500,000 or more depending on your business revenue, credit profile, and time in business. Unlike a term loan, a line of credit keeps working capital available on demand — a critical advantage for businesses with fluctuating revenue cycles.

5. Equipment Financing

If your business relies on machinery, vehicles, technology, or specialized tools, equipment financing lets you acquire what you need without depleting your cash reserves. The equipment itself serves as collateral, which often makes approval easier and faster than unsecured loans.

Equipment financing through ROK Financial can cover virtually any type of business equipment, from restaurant kitchen appliances to construction machinery to medical devices. Amounts range from $5,000 to several million dollars, with terms that align with the useful life of the equipment.

6. Merchant Cash Advance (MCA)

A merchant cash advance is not technically a loan — it's an advance against your future sales. A lender provides you with a lump sum of capital in exchange for a percentage of your daily or weekly credit card and debit card sales until the advance is repaid. MCAs are fast, flexible, and accessible even for businesses with less-than-perfect credit.

MCAs are best suited for businesses with consistent daily sales volume — restaurants, retail stores, e-commerce businesses, and service providers with high transaction volume. Approvals can happen in 24 hours or less, and funding is often deposited the same or next day.

7. Invoice Factoring

If your business invoices clients and waits 30, 60, or 90 days for payment, invoice factoring can unlock that cash immediately. You sell your outstanding invoices to a factoring company at a slight discount, and they advance you 80% to 95% of the invoice value right away. When your client pays, you receive the remaining balance minus the factoring fee.

This is an outstanding solution for B2B businesses, contractors, staffing agencies, and service providers who have strong clients but suffer from slow payment cycles. Invoice factoring doesn't require perfect credit — it's based on your clients' creditworthiness, not yours.

8. Revenue-Based Financing

Revenue-based financing (RBF) provides capital in exchange for a fixed percentage of your monthly revenue until a set repayment cap is reached. Unlike traditional loans, there's no fixed monthly payment — your repayment scales with your income. If revenue dips, your payment dips. If revenue surges, you pay off faster.

RBF is popular among SaaS companies, subscription businesses, e-commerce brands, and any business with consistent, trackable monthly revenue. It's a growth-friendly alternative to equity financing and doesn't require giving up ownership in your business.

9. Commercial Real Estate Loans

Ready to stop paying rent and start building equity? Commercial real estate loans allow business owners to purchase, refinance, or improve commercial property. Whether you're buying an office building, a retail location, a warehouse, or a mixed-use property, CRE loans provide the long-term capital you need to make the investment.

Commercial real estate financing is available for properties of all sizes, from small storefronts to large industrial facilities. Loan amounts can start at $100,000 and scale into the tens of millions for larger projects. Terms typically range from 10 to 25 years.

10. Unsecured Business Loans

Unsecured business loans require no collateral — approval is based on your business revenue, credit profile, and financial health. They're faster to process than secured loans and ideal for businesses that don't want to pledge assets or don't have significant collateral to offer.

Unsecured loans through ROK Financial's network can range from $10,000 to $500,000 depending on your qualifications. They work well for working capital, marketing campaigns, hiring, or any operational need where speed matters.

11. Bad Credit Business Loans

Poor credit doesn't have to be a dead end. Bad credit business loans are designed for business owners who have faced financial setbacks — whether from personal credit issues, past business challenges, or limited credit history. These products typically look more at business revenue and cash flow than personal credit scores.

Don't let a low credit score stop you from applying. ROK Financial works with businesses across the credit spectrum. Many business owners are surprised to find they qualify for more than they expected. Check your options today — it takes just minutes and won't affect your credit score.

Options for bad credit businesses include merchant cash advances, revenue-based financing, invoice factoring, and certain short-term loan products. The key is working with a funding specialist who understands your situation and can match you to the right product.
