When a business owner needs capital, the first instinct is often to look toward an SBA loan. And while SBA loans offer excellent rates, they aren't always the best fit for every situation—especially when speed is the deciding factor in securing a growth opportunity.

SBA vs. Alternative Lending: The Comparison

Which one is right for your business? It depends on your priorities:

SBA Loans

  • Pros: Lowest interest rates, longest repayment terms, government-backed security.
  • Cons: Intense documentation requirements, strict credit criteria, and a funding timeline that can stretch for months.
  • Best for: Real estate purchases, long-term debt refinancing, and situations where timing isn't critical.

Alternative Lending (ROK Financial)

  • Pros: Extreme speed (often 24-48 hours), flexible credit requirements, and minimal documentation.
  • Cons: Shorter repayment terms and higher rates compared to SBA.
  • Best for: Moving on time-sensitive opportunities, bridge funding, inventory purchases, and businesses that don't fit the "traditional" bank box.
